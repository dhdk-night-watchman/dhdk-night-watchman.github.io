The Night Watchman — design dossier

Design & Diagrams

A hybrid museum game for the Rijksmuseum, after dark. This page collects the concept, the game loop, and the three required diagrams: user flow, swimlane and user journey.

Concept

A museum night guard — the Night Watchman — lets the player wander the Rijksmuseum after closing. He guides them by lantern through four dark galleries, revealing real objects from the museum's open collection and asking a question or two about each. A wrong answer is never punished: the Watchman hints, then lets the player study the object or move on. Passive browsing becomes a guided, forgiving tour.

Game loop

Enter the dark hall → choose a gallery → sweep the lantern to find an object → answer a question → if wrong, get a hint and retry → if still unsure, study the information or skip → next question / object → gallery complete → back to the hall. In short: Light → Find → Question → Hint/Help → Advance.

Figure 1. The play flow. The forgiving branch (hint → retry → reveal → study-or-skip) guarantees there is no dead end: a wrong answer always leads forward.

Hybrid layer (physical space)

The web game is the digital half. The physical half is a small darkened gallery that recreates the "museum after dark": framed objects sit on the walls in shadow, and the visitor carries a real handheld lantern (or uses their phone as a torch). Sweeping the light over a frame triggers the same question on a nearby screen or on their phone. The room is modelled in Blender / CAD and drawn by hand; the interaction between lantern, frame and screen is documented in the diagrams above. Together the dark room + the digital layer make the experience hybrid.

Built on real Rijksmuseum Collection Online records. Guide: the Night Watchman.
